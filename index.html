INTERESTING facts about CAMEL

  • camels are of two types :-
  • Dromedary or Arabian Camels are usually found in the Middle East. They are the camels with 1 hump.
  • Bacterian camel are usually found in parts of central Asia . These are the camels having 2 humps.
  • Camels can survive harsh climate. They can survive a long time without food and water

INTERESTING facts about CHAMELEON

  1. Chameleons live in warm varied habitats from rainforests through to deserts.
  2. Chameleons are not deaf but they do not actually have ear openings.
  3. Chameleons actually change their colour due to a number of different factors, including their mood, changes in light or temperature, or the humidity of their environment.
  4. Unlike other lizards, a chameleon's tail will not grow back if broken off.

INTERESTING facts about PIGEON

  1. Pigeons have strong muscles used for flying. They can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet.
  2. Pigeons are highly intelligent animals. They are able to recognize themselves in the mirror.
  3. Pigeons have exceptional eyesight and ability to identify objects on a distance of 26 miles.
  4. Pigeons can survive more than 30 years in the wild.

INTERESTING facts about TEDDY-BEAR

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. September 9th is National Teddy Bear Day!
  3. The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
  4. The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

INTERESTING facts about PANDA

  1. Giant Pandas are known around the world for their unique black and white appearance.
  2. Pandas are BIG eaters – every day they fill their tummies for up to 12 hours, shifting up to 12 kilograms of bamboo!
  3. Baby pandas are born pink and measure about 15cm.
  4. Panda cubs can climb trees at just the young age of six months!

INTERESTING facts about CICADA

  1. Cicada are around 2.500 cicadas that can be found on all continents except on the Antarctica.
  2. Cicadas spend most of their life under the ground.
  3. Cicadas are one of the loudest insects on the planet. They produce sound of up to 120 dB that can be heard 0.5 miles away.
  4. Cicadas are herbivores. Their diet is based on the juices extracted from the root and stem of various plants.
